Customising

Making an article unique

Make each page special (just like this one!)

You can tweak the look of an article if you have some special content requirements. For example, you might have an image gallery of strong black and white images, and wish to put them on a dark background. Or you want to have more colourful page titles, or change around the fonts.

To customise an article, go to the Design tab and change around the values to suite. The colours and fonts are defined on the right of the form, and are based on the theme you have chosen.

You can customise based on your selected theme

What can I change?

  1. Article Background - this is the background color of the entire article.
  2. Heading Background - the background colour of the heading block.
  3. Headline - the main title - you can change the colour, font used, and style - bold, italic, bold italic , ALL CAPS, or ALL CAPS BOLD
  4. Subheadline - the secondary title - you can change the colour, font used, and style - bold, italic, bold italic , ALL CAPS, or ALL CAPS BOLD
  5. Credits - the article credits in the heading - you can change the font and colour.
  6. Body Text - the text of the article. You can change the font and colour.
  7. Link Colours - the colour of links. As with the body text, be sure to choose colours that contrast with the article background colour.

Once you are finished, hit Save Draft and select the Preview tab to see how it looks.

Secret Mode

You can add inline styles by placing it within a <style>...</style> block in the head of the content. (Your milage may vary with this approach).
