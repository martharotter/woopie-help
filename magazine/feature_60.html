Getting Started

Make your first publication

Words
Stewart Curry

How to make a new publication in three easy steps...

Our publications work like this - you have a publication, which is made up of issues, which are made up of articles.

Step 1: Create a New Publication

How to create a publication

A publication is what we call your title - it can be a regular publication that comes out every month, or just a one-off publication. You publication is your title, for example The Monthly Movie Review or The Weekly Company Newsletter.

To create your publication, select New Publication from the dashboard (just after you log in) and fill in the details. You'll need:

  1. A title - e.g. Cupcakes Monthly
  2. A tagline - e.g. All the latest Cupcakes news
  3. A logo or thumbnail to help you identify your publication on the listings page
  4. A URL format
  5. A visual theme. (the theme defines the colours and typography used in your publication)

Once you've added in the publication details, hit Create Publication and you're done! Now you've defined your publication, it's time to add your first issue.

Once you've created your publication, click Edit Publication in the Articles listing page to add extra details such as your Twitter, Google Analytics and Facebook IDs for social sharing and tracking.

Step 2: Create your first Issue

Creating the first issue

An issue is one edition or volume of your publication - for example if your publication was Cupcakes Monthly then an issue would be Cupcakes for Summer. Each issue is a discreet bundle that we package for you in lots of different formats for you to send to your readers.

To create a new issue, first select your publication from the Dashboard, then click New Issue at the top right.

Now add in the publication details - you'll need to add:

  1. The title (for that particular issue) - e.g. Cupcakes for Summer
  2. The slug (for the URL) - e.g. summer-cupcakes
  3. The volume and issue numbers (these should be digits not words, i.e. 2 not two)
  4. A cover image thumbnail for your reference.

The hit Create Issue and we'll start adding articles!

Step 3: Add some articles and Publish!

Adding a Cover

Using Templates

We use different templates for different types of content. One of the first templates you'll want to add is a "Cover" template. This is the 'landing page' for web readers, and is a great way to introduce your issue and give it some branding and a theme.

Each issue is made up of articles - each article is a distinct part of that issue, on one particular topic. (We don't really like the word page, as a page on the web is different than a page in iBooks and it can get confusing).

An example of an article (continuing our cupcakes publication theme) would be "Recipe for Vanilla Cupcakes" or "Image Gallery: Beautiful Toppings"

You create an article by clicking (+) Add an Article or New Article on the Articles page. You'll then be asked to choose a template - we'll cover these in more detail but for know just select Cover.

Describe

We have four parts to a template. First you describe the template by filling in some details and uploading some images. Some fields must be filled in - these are marked with a red asterisk *. We need:

  1. A thumbnail image for your reference
  2. Background - this image covers the whole background of the cover
  3. Main image - this image defaults to the centre of the cover, and works best as a transparent PNG if you are using it for a logo.
  4. Alt attributes - describe the images for visually impaired readers.
  5. Description - This is great for social sharing (for example, for posting to Facebook) and SEO
  6. Status - use this to track the progress of creating the article

Hit Save Draft once you're done.

Compose

You can add headlines to your cover if you want to make a magazine-style cover. Hit Save Draft once you're done.

Design

You can change the font, colour and style of each headline, and also reposition the main image. Hit Save Draft once you're done.

Preview

You can see how your cover looks at a range of sizes by selecting different sizes under the "Preview" tab.

All done? Return to the Article Listing page via the Breadcrumbs at the top of the page, and hit Generate Web at the bottom. Your publication is now Published!
