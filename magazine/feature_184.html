Your First Feature

Adding your first feature

Words
Stewart Curry

The feature template is one of our most flexible and powerful template - you'll probably use it the most. Features are great for longer reviews, interviews, short stories, in fact any type of long-form narrative content. You can add media elements to features (such as pull quotes, side images etc) to features to help with the layout and narrative flow, and to keep things interesting for the reader.

Adding content to your feature article

Creating a Feature

To add a Feature article, select (+) Add an Article from the Issue article listing screen, and choose Feature as a template.

Describe

The first thing you'll need to do is Describe your article - adding in the headlines, featured image and a few other items to your article to help describe what it's all about.

  1. Title - a working title for you to reference
  2. Headline - the headline of your article
  3. Subheadline
  4. A thumbnail image for contents and social sharing
  5. A Featured Image - a big bold main image for the article
  6. A Description - this is a summary which we use in the contents, on Facebook, and a few other places. It's good for SEO too.

Hit Save Draft once you're done and we'll add some content.

Content

Now you can add some content to your article. Just start typing into the Content field. We use Markdown to describe content - there's some help on the side and we've more details on it later.

You can also add Media Figures from the drop-down underneath - just choose the type you want, fill in the details, and then paste the shortcode in the Content field where you want that object to appear. (We have more on Media Figures later)

Happy with your content? Hit Save Draft to save your progress. You can tweak the look of the page via the Design tab, and see how it looks with the Preview tab. (You can even see how your article looks in a browser by clicking Open in new tab)

All done? Return to the Article Listing page via the Breadcrumbs at the top of the page, and hit Generate Web at the bottom. Your publication is now Published!
